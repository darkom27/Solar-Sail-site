theoretical static solar mirror concept proposed to generate net momentum on the Solar Syste

## Analytics Covered


### Engineering Analysis Page
- Newton's Third Law at photon level (p = E/c, P = 2I/c)
- Force balance calculation (required areal density ≈ 1.54 mg/m²)
- Material comparison table (graphene, aluminium, beryllium, lunar glass, metamaterials)
- Thermodynamic collapse model (ΔT/Δt ≈ 90,000 K/s for 1 mg/m² mirror)
- Photon-surface and photon-photon (QED) interaction effects


### Realistic Constraints Page
- Lunar regolith mirror specifications (100 m diam., 64 μm thick, 1.78 t, 60% reflectivity)
- 3 transfer waypoints: 1.96 AU (⚠), 1.4 AU (⛔), 2.8 AU (✅)
- 3 interactive charts: Temperature vs AU, Gravity vs AU, Photon Flux vs AU
- Scale problem: 6.21 mN total thrust, ~10²⁰ mirrors needed for practical effect
